Recommended fund shift at retirement — age 57
Why shift at retirement?
Your situation is unique. With two guaranteed pensions (~$77K/yr floor) you do not need to go fully conservative. The pensions cover essentials — the portfolio is lifestyle money. A moderate allocation maintains growth to outpace inflation while reducing sequence-of-returns risk in early retirement years.

The key risk at retirement is not running out of money — it is a major market crash in the first 3–5 years of retirement depleting the portfolio before it can recover. A moderate shift reduces that exposure without abandoning growth entirely.

US core (VTI 25%) — Total US market anchor. Broader than VOO, most tax-efficient equity ETF available. Higher weight than personal M1 since no TSP C Fund to anchor US exposure.
International core (VXUS 20%) — Total international in one fund (developed + EM). Provides broad foundation before factor tilts layer on top. Personal M1 uses AVDV only; kid's account needs the broader base.
Factor tilt (AVUV 25% + AVDV 20% + AVES 10%) — Avantis funds targeting Size, Value, and Profitability. 55% factor-tilted vs 45% core — appropriate for 10–20+ year horizon with no withdrawal pressure.
Key difference from personal M1: No QQQM (tech concentration not needed — no TSP to balance against). Higher VTI (standalone account needs more US core). VXUS instead of VEA/VWO split (simpler, more teachable as child matures).
